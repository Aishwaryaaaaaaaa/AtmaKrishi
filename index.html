The Idea

WHO WE ARE

We are BAM — a team of 11 students from YEB building AtmaKrishi to preserve India's indigenous agricultural knowledge before it is lost forever.

OUR BELIEF

Every farming community holds generations of wisdom that cannot be replaced by data alone. By combining verified traditional knowledge with AI, we can help farmers make smarter decisions while preserving India's agricultural heritage.

THE PROBLEM

India is Losing Its Agricultural Wisdom.

Undocumented Knowledge

Thousands of indigenous farming practices exist only in memory, with no structured archive to preserve them.

Generational Loss

As experienced farmers retire, decades of practical wisdom disappear along with them.

Vanishing Biodiversity

Traditional seeds and region-specific cultivation techniques are steadily being replaced and forgotten.

One-Size-Fits-All Advice

Generic recommendations often overlook local climate, soil conditions, and regional farming traditions.

WHY THIS MATTERS

Numbers don't lie. The clock is ticking.

110,000 → 6,000

Rice varieties lost

India once cultivated over 1,10,000 traditional rice varieties. Today, only a few thousand remain in fields — the rest exist only in gene banks or not at all.

National Bureau of Plant Genetic Resources (ICAR)
100%

Potash import dependence

India imports its entire potash requirement and a large share of phosphatic fertilisers, leaving millions of farmers exposed to global price shocks.

Ministry of Chemicals & Fertilisers, 2023–24
60+

Age of knowledge holders

Most traditional farming knowledge resides with farmers aged 60 and above. Without urgent documentation, this oral wisdom will vanish within one generation.

NSSO 77th Round & MSSRF field surveys
500M+

WhatsApp users in India

With over 500 million active users and high rural penetration, WhatsApp voice notes are already the de‑facto communication tool for farmer groups.

IAMAI-Kantar, TRAI 2023
25M

Annual calls to Kisan Call Centre

Over 25 million calls per year prove that voice-based advisory is the most trusted and accessible format for Indian farmers.

Dept. of Agriculture, Cooperation & Farmers Welfare
₹2,400 Cr

Digital Agriculture Mission outlay
